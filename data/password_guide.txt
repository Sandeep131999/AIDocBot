PASSWORD SECURITY AND RESET GUIDE
===================================

SECTION 1: HOW TO RESET YOUR PASSWORD
--------------------------------------

If you have forgotten your password or want to change it, follow these steps:

Step 1: Visit the login page
  - Go to https://example.com/login
  - Do NOT enter any password yet

Step 2: Click "Forgot Password"
  - Look for a link that says "Forgot Password?" or "Can't sign in?"
  - Click on this link

Step 3: Enter your email address
  - Type the email address associated with your account
  - Click "Next" or "Send Reset Link"

Step 4: Check your email
  - Open your email inbox
  - Look for an email from "security@example.com"
  - Subject: "Password Reset Request"
  - If not there, check your spam folder

Step 5: Click the reset link
  - Open the email
  - Click the link that says "Reset Password"
  - This link expires in 24 hours

Step 6: Create a new password
  - Your new password must have:
    • At least 12 characters
    • At least one uppercase letter (A-Z)
    • At least one lowercase letter (a-z)
    • At least one number (0-9)
    • At least one special character (!@#$%^&*)
  
  Example of strong password: MyNewPass2024!

Step 7: Confirm your password
  - Type your new password again in the "Confirm Password" field
  - Make sure both match exactly

Step 8: Click "Save New Password"
  - Your password is now changed
  - You will be logged out automatically
  - Log in again with your new password

SECTION 2: PASSWORD BEST PRACTICES
-----------------------------------

1. Never share your password
   - Support staff will never ask for your password
   - Don't type passwords in emails or messages

2. Use a unique password
   - Don't use the same password on multiple websites
   - If one site gets hacked, others stay safe

3. Use a password manager
   - Tools like Bitwarden, 1Password, or LastPass
   - Store all your passwords safely
   - Generate strong random passwords

4. Enable two-factor authentication
   - Adds extra security beyond password
   - Even if someone gets your password, they can't access your account
   - See section 3 below

5. Change password if:
   - You suspect someone knows it
   - You used it on a website that was hacked
   - You haven't changed it in 6 months
   - You shared it with someone

SECTION 3: TWO-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION (2FA)
-------------------------------------------

What is 2FA?
Two-factor authentication means you need TWO things to log in:
1. Your password (something you know)
2. A code from your phone (something you have)

Even if someone steals your password, they can't log in without the code.

How to enable 2FA:
1. Go to Account Settings
2. Click "Security"
3. Click "Enable Two-Factor Authentication"
4. Choose your preferred method:
   - SMS: Get a code via text message
   - Email: Get a code via email
   - Authenticator app: Use Google Authenticator or Authy

5. Follow the setup wizard
6. Save your backup codes in a safe place

SECTION 4: ACCOUNT RECOVERY
----------------------------

Lost access to your account?

Recovery methods (in order):
1. Email recovery
   - The email address on your account
   - You must have access to this email

2. Phone recovery
   - The phone number you provided
   - We can send a verification code

3. Backup codes
   - Generated when you enabled 2FA
   - Store these safely

4. Contact support
   - Email: support@example.com
   - We'll verify your identity and help you regain access
   - Recovery usually takes 24-48 hours

Important: We can NEVER restore your old password.
We can only send you a reset link to create a new password.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
---------------------------

Q: How long is the password reset link valid?
A: 24 hours. After that, you need to request a new link.

Q: Can I reset my password on my phone?
A: Yes, the process is the same on any device.

Q: What if I don't receive the reset email?
A: Check your spam folder. If still not there, contact support.

Q: Can someone else change my password?
A: No, they would need access to your email to use the reset link.

Q: How often should I change my password?
A: Every 6 months is recommended, or if you suspect compromise.

Q: Is my password encrypted?
A: Yes, we use industry-standard encryption. We cannot see your password.

Q: What if I forget my new password right after changing it?
A: You can use the "Forgot Password" process again.
